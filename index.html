Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026
🔒

Our Privacy Commitment (Zero-Data Architecture)

ShieldCall is built on a "Zero-Data" architecture. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers. All processing is performed strictly on your device. Your phone numbers, contacts, and call information never leave your device. ShieldCall has no backend servers, no user accounts, and no cloud storage. Your data belongs entirely to you.

📞

How ShieldCall Works (On-Device Processing)

ShieldCall utilizes the Android Call Screening Service to analyze incoming calls in real-time before your phone rings.

  • Local Filtering: Incoming numbers are checked against your local Blocked List and Trusted Contacts.
  • Spam Signal Analysis: We utilize your carrier's network-level signals (STIR/SHAKEN) and local number pattern analysis to identify potential fraud.
  • Privacy-First: Calls that are allowed through are never recorded, logged, or monitored. ShieldCall does not interact with your system's native call history.
📋

Data Stored Locally (Room Database)

To provide core functionality, ShieldCall stores the following in a secure local database protected by Android's app sandbox on your device:

  • Blocked & Trusted Lists: Numbers you manually manage, including custom labels.
  • Blocked Call Records: A log of blocked attempts (Number, Name, and Reason) so you can review activity.
  • User Control: You can clear all logs at any time. You may also export your blocked list as a local file for backup; this file remains under your control and is never shared with us.
👤

Contacts Access

ShieldCall requests the READ_CONTACTS permission solely to identify known callers and prevent them from being accidentally blocked.

  • Real-Time Only: The check happens in-memory during an incoming call.
  • No Uploads: Your contact list is never uploaded, synced, or shared. It is used only to cross-reference the incoming caller ID against your address book locally.
⚙️

Permissions Disclosure

We follow the principle of least privilege, requesting only what is essential:

  • Call Screening Role: The core requirement for blocking calls on modern Android versions (API 29+).
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS: Optional permission (Android 13+) used to alert you of a blocked call.
  • INTERNET: Used strictly by the Google AdMob SDK to load advertisements.

Users can manage or revoke these permissions at any time through the 'Manage Notifications' or 'Call Screening' options within the app's Settings menu, which provide direct links to the Android System Settings.

📢

Advertising & Third-Party Data

ShieldCall displays ads provided by Google AdMob. To serve relevant ads, the AdMob SDK may collect limited device information, such as Advertising IDs. This data collection is performed by Google and is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. ShieldCall does not share your contacts, call logs, or blocked numbers with Google or third-party advertisers.

🛡️

Security & Data Privacy

Your data is protected by the Android App Sandbox, ensuring that ShieldCall's information is isolated from other apps. Because we do not use remote servers, there is no risk of a centralized data breach.

Data Deletion: ShieldCall stores your blocklists and encrypted call log of blocked calls locally. In accordance with Android's storage standards, this local database is cleared by the system when the app is uninstalled. Users can also manually clear all data at any time through the app settings.

👶

Children's Privacy

ShieldCall is not directed toward children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from any users, including children.
