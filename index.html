🛡️ ShieldCall

Privacy Policy

🔒 No Data Collection

ShieldCall does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers.

All processing is performed entirely on your device. Your phone numbers, contacts, and call information never leave your device by ShieldCall.

ShieldCall has no backend servers, no user accounts, and no cloud storage. Your data belongs to you.

📞 How ShieldCall Works

ShieldCall uses Android's built-in Call Screening Service to screen and analyze incoming calls in real time before they reach you.

When a call comes in, ShieldCall checks it against your blocked numbers list, your trusted contacts, and spam detection signals — all on-device. If the call is identified as unwanted, it is silently rejected before your phone rings.

Call data is processed in real time and is not stored unless a call is blocked.

ShieldCall does not access, read, or interact with your device's native phone call history or system call log in any way.

📋 What ShieldCall Stores Locally

ShieldCall stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Blocked numbers list — numbers you have manually blocked, with optional labels
  • Trusted numbers list — numbers you have explicitly marked as trusted
  • Blocked call records — a log of calls ShieldCall has blocked, including the number and reason for blocking
  • App settings — your preferences such as spam blocking, trusted contacts toggle, and notification preference
  • Block statistics — lifetime, daily, and monthly blocked call counts

Calls that are allowed through are never recorded or stored. Only blocked calls appear in the in-app log.

All of this data remains exclusively on your device and is never transmitted externally. You can clear the blocked call log at any time from within the app.

👤 Contacts Access

ShieldCall requests access to your contacts solely to identify trusted callers and prevent accidental blocking of people you know.

This data is processed locally on your device and is never uploaded, shared, transmitted, or stored outside your device. It is used for no other purpose.

⚙️ Permissions

ShieldCall requests only the permissions strictly necessary for its core functionality:

  • Call Screening Role — required by Android to screen and block incoming calls before they ring. Granted through Android's system role manager, not as a standard runtime permission.
  • READ_CONTACTS — used locally to identify known contacts and avoid accidentally blocking trusted callers. Data is processed on-device only and never transmitted.
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS — used to notify you when a call has been blocked. You are asked to grant or deny this permission when you first open the app.
  • INTERNET — required solely for Google AdMob to load and display advertisements.

ShieldCall does not request or use READ_CALL_LOG, READ_PHONE_STATE, READ_PHONE_NUMBERS, ANSWER_PHONE_CALLS, or any other sensitive telephony permissions beyond the Call Screening role.

🔔 Notifications

ShieldCall can notify you when a call has been blocked. This permission is optional.

If you decline, no notifications will be sent. You can enable or disable notifications at any time in your device settings.

🛡️ Security

All data stored by ShieldCall is kept locally on your device using Android's built-in app sandbox and security protections.

Because ShieldCall uses no external servers and requires no user account, there is no remote access to your data.

📢 Advertising

ShieldCall displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect limited device information — such as advertising identifiers — to serve relevant ads.

This data collection is performed by Google and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy:
https://policies.google.com/privacy

ShieldCall itself does not collect, access, or share any personal data with advertisers or third parties.

👶 Children's Privacy

ShieldCall is not directed toward children under the age of 13.

The app does not knowingly collect personal data from anyone, including children. Since no personal data is collected by the app itself, no data from children is stored or processed.
